Active Studies/Research

Automatic species classification and trait segmentation from specimen images

We are developing biology-guided neural networks (BGNNs), a new class of artificial neural networks that leverage structured biological knowledge from anatomy ontologies and phylogenies in the learning of artificial neural networks. These BGNNs will automatically classify species and detect anatomical traits on specimen images.

Automated metadata quality assessment and provenance tracking

We are developing tools for assessment and tracking for specimen image data collected from diverse heterogeneous sources. We will also create approaches to semantically integrate discovered traits with existing knowledgebases, while automatically tracking object history (provenance).

Discovery of novel biological knowledge

We are interesting in developing new ontological relationships among traits, as well as previously undiscovered traits, and complete and accurate trait distributions over species.
